## 👋 About Me

I'm **Rongkai Bao (包戎楷)**, a B.Med. student in Clinical Medicine at **Huaqiao University** (graduating June 2026), with a strong passion for **translational toxicology**, **computational biology**, and **systems medicine**.

### 🎯 Research Interests

My research focuses on understanding **chemical-induced developmental toxicity** and its underlying molecular mechanisms. I combine:

- **Experimental toxicology**: Zebrafish models, developmental biology
- **Omics analytics**: Bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq), single-cell ATAC-seq (scATAC-seq)
- **Computational approaches**: Molecular docking, molecular dynamics simulations, network analysis
- **Mathematical modeling**: Agent-based modeling, systems biology

**Specific Research Areas**:
1. **Pesticide toxicity mechanisms** - Understanding how agrochemicals disrupt developmental programs
2. **Multi-omics integration** - Linking transcriptomic and epigenomic changes to phenotypic outcomes
3. **Computational screening** - Predicting chemical safety profiles using structural and systems approaches
4. **Network medicine** - Identifying key regulatory nodes in toxicity pathways

## 🌟 Philosophy & Approach

I believe that **rigorous science thrives at the intersection of disciplines**. My work embodies this philosophy:

- **Experimental rigor** meets **computational creativity**
- **Mechanistic understanding** drives **clinical application**
- **Quantitative thinking** guides **biological interpretation**

I'm committed to reproducible research, open science, and mentoring the next generation of scientists. Whether through publications, code repositories, or collaborative projects, I aim to contribute meaningfully to the biomedical research community.

## 🎓 Why This Path?

Growing up, I was fascinated by **how things work** — whether it's a machine, a cell, or a complex biological system. This curiosity led me to medicine and then to research.

I chose toxicology because it sits at a critical intersection:
- **Environmental health** (protecting public health from chemical exposure)
- **Mechanistic biology** (understanding fundamental processes)
- **Computational innovation** (leveraging AI and big data)

As I transition to my PhD, I'm expanding my perspective to include **hematologic malignancies** and **regenerative medicine**, bringing the same integrative approach to systemic diseases.
